# MESS-Materials v0.2 — Producer Plan

_Status: draft, authored 2026-04-20. Successor to v0.1.0-pilot
(tagged ba02342). Consumer-side counterpart will mirror to
messai-ai:docs/plans/mess-materials-v0.2.md once this is merged._

## Goal

Add **depth** (MP fields that directly feed the Electrode Viability,
Long-Term Stability, and Biofilm Compatibility scores shipped in the
strategy) and one piece of **breadth** (MXene coverage, currently TEMP
unmapped), plus two **MES-specific novel** capabilities that no
materials database offers on its own: (a) material ↔ paper
cross-reference joining against MESS-Parameters, and (b) substitution
candidates filtered by MES-operating-condition stability.

Nothing in v0.2 requires new external APIs beyond MP + the already-
pinned MESS-Parameters submodule.

## Scope — the six deliverables

Ordered by ingestion dependency, not priority:

### 1. Elasticity (depth)

**Source:** `mpr.materials.elasticity.search(material_ids=[...])`.
Coverage: ~13k MP materials (all of our 8 current unique mp_ids are in
scope; spot-check expected coverage before committing).

**New fields (per material record in rich.json):**
- `elasticity.bulk_modulus_GPa` (K_VRH average) — nullable
- `elasticity.shear_modulus_GPa` (G_VRH average) — nullable
- `elasticity.youngs_modulus_GPa` (derived from K, G) — nullable
- `elasticity.poissons_ratio` (`homogeneous_poisson`) — nullable
- `elasticity.universal_anisotropy` — nullable
- `elasticity.debye_temperature_K` (if available) — nullable
- `elasticity.source_functional` (PBE|PBE+U) — string

**MES relevance:** bulk modulus below 50 GPa → flag "fragile — monitor
for mechanical failure" in high-flow reactors. Bulk modulus above 100
GPa → "high durability." Feeds the Long-Term Stability Score mechanical
facet.

**Script:** `scripts/fetch-elasticity.py` (new). Caches under
`data/mp-cache-elasticity/<mp_id>.json`. Same --refresh pattern as
fetch-mp.py.

**Risk:** ~20% of our mp_ids may lack elasticity data (graphite,
specific oxide phases). Missing = nullable fields, no fallback.

### 2. Surface energies + work function (depth)

**Source:** `mpr.materials.surface_properties.search(material_ids=[...])`.
Coverage: ~1k MP materials (lower than elasticity; expect gaps).

**New fields:**
- `surface.weighted_surface_energy_J_per_m2` — nullable
- `surface.weighted_work_of_adhesion_J_per_m2` — nullable
- `surface.shape_factor` — nullable
- `surface.surface_anisotropy` — nullable
- `surface.work_function_eV` — nullable; separate MP field but same call
- `surface.facets` — optional array of per-facet `{miller: [h,k,l],
  surface_energy_J_per_m2, area_fraction}` for materials where per-
  facet detail is warranted (primarily the Pt/MnO2/Fe3O4 catalyst
  cathodes)

**MES relevance:**
- Surface energy feeds Biofilm Compatibility Score (40% weight;
  literature data still weighted 60% per prior feedback).
- Work function is a direct ORR/OER activity proxy for cathode
  catalysts — Pt's ~5.7 eV is the benchmark; Co3O4, MnO2, Fe3O4 work
  functions are what distinguish their catalytic behavior.

**Script:** `scripts/fetch-surfaces.py`. Cache dir
`data/mp-cache-surfaces/`.

**Risk:** Low MP coverage for some oxides. For carbon family (all
proxied to graphite), only mp-48's surface energy is available —
same amorphous caveat already in SCIENTIFIC_INTEGRITY.md applies.

### 3. MXene coverage (breadth)

Currently TEMP unmapped: `ti3c2tx_mxene`, `nb2ctx_mxene`, `v2ctx_mxene`.

**Approach:**
1. Search MP for entries matching Ti₃C₂, Nb₂C, V₂C stoichiometries.
   MP has MAX-phase parents (Ti₃AlC₂, V₂AlC, Nb₂AlC) and some pure-
   MXene structures. Verify each via API query, record both
   parent-MAX-phase and pure-MXene mp_ids.
2. For MXenes where MP lacks the pure phase, fall back to MAX-phase
   parent with `proxy: true` and an explicit note; do NOT silently
   map to MAX.
3. If both are missing, leave as unmapped with reason "MP lacks
   crystalline MXene coverage; C2DB integration deferred to v0.3."

**Deliverable:** updated `slug-to-mp.yaml` entries for the 3 MXenes
(either mapped or with non-TEMP reasons), plus a new
`data/mxene-coverage.md` summarizing the lookup results for
reproducibility.

**Decision point:** if all three MXenes map cleanly to MP entries
with confidence_tier ≥ medium, drop the "MXene follow-up" section of
SCIENTIFIC_INTEGRITY.md. If any remain proxied to MAX parents, keep
the caveat and document which mechanism (termination ambiguity,
layer count, etc.) the proxy cannot capture.

### 4. Stainless-steel alloy refinement (breadth)

Currently `stainless_steel → mp-13 (Fe)` with passivator override.
That's defensible for v0.1.0-pilot but misses Cr/Ni content which is
the whole point of stainless.

**Approach:** MP has several Fe-Cr-Ni alloy structures that approximate
SS-304 (18-8) and SS-316 (18-10-2). Survey MP for:
- Fe-Cr binary structures near x_Cr = 0.18
- Fe-Cr-Ni ternary structures near (0.70, 0.18, 0.08) and (0.68, 0.17,
  0.10, 0.02 Mo) [SS-316 has Mo]

Update slug-to-mp.yaml to use a multi-component mapping:
```yaml
- slug: stainless_steel
  components:
    - mp_id: <best Fe-Cr-Ni mp_id>
      role: bulk_structure
      proxy: true  # still a proxy: real SS has additional C, Mn, Si
    - mp_id: mp-25471  # Cr (for passivator contribution)
      role: dopant
```

**Risk:** Stainless-like ternary structures in MP may be high-
energy-above-hull (theoretical only). Accept up to ~50 meV/atom
above hull with explicit note.

### 5. Material ↔ paper cross-reference (novel, MES-specific)

**This is the item no other materials database has and the one most
likely to become the "why researchers care about MESS-Materials"
hook.**

**Source:** MESS-Parameters' pinned submodule:
- `data/paper-parameter-values.csv` — which parameters were measured in
  which papers.
- `data/paper-metadata.csv` — paper identifiers, titles, DOIs.
- `data/extracted-parameter-data.csv` — per-paper extracted values.

**Approach:**
1. For each MESS-Materials slug (e.g. `carbon_cloth`), find all papers
   in `paper-parameter-values.csv` where that slug appears as a
   measured parameter.
2. For each such paper, collect the other parameters measured in that
   paper — the "co-occurrence set" — especially performance fields
   (power_density, coulombic_efficiency, current_density).
3. Aggregate per-slug into a summary object:

```yaml
paper_cross_reference:
  paper_count: 32
  paper_ids: [...]  # first 10 only; full list in a separate file
  co_measured_performance:
    power_density_mW_per_m2:
      n: 28
      mean: 812.3
      median: 450.0
      std: 1042.7        # will be huge — document the spread
      p10: 85
      p90: 2400
    coulombic_efficiency_pct: { ... }
    current_density_A_per_m2: { ... }
```

**New data artifact:**
- `data/material-paper-crossref.json` — full per-slug paper list + the
  co-measured performance summary. Large enough to warrant its own
  file; not embedded in rich.json.

**New field in rich.json (compact summary only):**
- `paper_cross_reference.paper_count`
- `paper_cross_reference.performance_summary` (top 3 metrics by n)

**UI value:** On the parameter detail page, a new subsection reads
"32 papers in the corpus used carbon cloth; median reported power
density 450 mW/m² (10th–90th: 85–2400)." Direct, concrete, novel.

**Script:** `scripts/build-paper-crossref.py`.

**Risk:** The MESS-Parameters `paper-parameter-values.csv` has not
been validated against all 687 parameter slugs, and the per-paper
parameter association for material-type parameters may be sparse or
inconsistent. Run a coverage check before committing; if <50% of
slugs have ≥3 papers, scope back to "top 10 highest-usage slugs only"
and document the gap upstream for MESS-Parameters curators.

### 6. Substitution candidates (novel, MES-specific)

For each mapped slug, surface up to 5 MP materials that:
- Have the same `is_metal` classification (or both have band_gap < 1 eV)
- Have Pourbaix state matching at MFC cathode conditions
- Have formation energy within ±0.5 eV/atom
- Contain at least one element not already in the source material
  (trivially promotes chemical diversity, rules out same material
  in different polymorphs)
- Score highest on (low cost per kg × low above-hull × high coverage
  in existing literature)

**Approach (v0.2 scope — heuristic, not ML):**
- Pull Tier 1 summary for ~200 candidate mp_ids in the
  Fe/Mn/Co/Ni/Ti/Cu/Mo/W/C/O chemsys (one-time batch fetch, cached).
- For each source slug, score and rank candidates per the criteria.
- Output `data/substitution-candidates.json` keyed by source slug.

**New field in rich.json:**
- `substitution_candidates`: array of `{mp_id, formula, rationale,
  est_cost_proxy}`, max 5.

**UI value:** "Platinum cathode — cost estimate $30,000/kg.
Substitution candidates in MESS-Materials: [Ag, Ni, Co3O4, activated
carbon, Fe3O4] ranked by (stability match × cost × existing MES
literature coverage)." Actionable for researchers trying to reduce
catalyst cost.

**Script:** `scripts/rank-substitutions.py`. Depends on #1, #2, #5.

**Risk:** "Cost estimate" is a rough proxy (elemental abundance + a
hardcoded premium for noble metals). Mark it as a proxy, not a real
market price; refine in v0.3 when a cost data source is identified.

## Schema bump

`schema_version: "0.1.0"` → `"0.2.0"`. Additive only — all v0.1.0
fields remain unchanged. Consumers pinned to v0.1.0-pilot continue
to work; v0.2.0 consumers opt in.

**Changes to `schemas/mp-material.schema.json`:**
- Top-level: allow `"0.2.0"` as valid `schema_version`; keep `"0.1.0"`
  also accepted for one release so downstream migration isn't
  blocking.
- Per-material: add optional objects `elasticity`, `surface`,
  `paper_cross_reference`, `substitution_candidates`. All nullable.
- No required-field additions.

**TS mirror update:** `apps/web/src/types/material-schema.ts` gets
nullable `elasticity`, `surface`, etc. shapes. Downstream UI either
renders the new sections when present or silently omits them.

## Ingestion ordering

```
Phase 1: API fetches + caches (independent, can run in parallel)
  ├── fetch-elasticity.py         (depth #1)
  ├── fetch-surfaces.py           (depth #2)
  └── fetch-substitution-pool.py  (breadth for #6)

Phase 2: Curation (human-in-the-loop, blocks on outputs above)
  ├── MXene MP lookup → slug-to-mp.yaml update (#3)
  └── Stainless alloy MP lookup → slug-to-mp.yaml update (#4)

Phase 3: Joins + derivations (dependent on Phases 1 + 2)
  ├── build-paper-crossref.py     (novel #5 — depends on nothing above)
  └── rank-substitutions.py       (novel #6 — depends on #1, #2, #5)

Phase 4: Assembly
  └── assemble-rich-json.py updated to emit schema 0.2.0 with new
      fields; schema validation tightened to reject unknown fields.

Phase 5: Tag v0.2.0
```

## Pipeline validation checklist (must pass before tag)

- [ ] All v0.1.0-pilot sanity anchors still pass byte-for-byte on the
      fields they covered (no regression in Tier 1 or Pourbaix).
- [ ] Elasticity coverage report: N of 8 unique mp_ids have
      elasticity data; nulls are explicit.
- [ ] Surface coverage report: N of 8 have surface data.
- [ ] MXene lookup documented: each of 3 MXenes is either mapped or
      has a non-TEMP unmapped reason.
- [ ] Stainless alloy refinement: slug-to-mp.yaml has a multi-
      component entry for `stainless_steel` OR a documented
      justification to keep the Fe-only proxy.
- [ ] Paper cross-reference coverage: ≥70% of 15 mapped slugs have
      paper_count ≥ 1. If below, scope down or document upstream gap.
- [ ] Substitution candidates: every mapped slug has ≥ 1 candidate
      (or an explicit "no viable substitutes" record).
- [ ] Schema v0.2.0 validates against the test rich.json;
      schema v0.1.0 still validates against the v0.1.0-pilot artifact.
- [ ] SCIENTIFIC_INTEGRITY.md extended with: elasticity/surface
      coverage caveats, substitution-score proxy warning, paper-
      cross-reference extraction-quality reminder (ties to
      MESS-Parameters CoV).

## Consumer-side mirror (messai-ai, Phase 4-lite)

Must land in messai-ai in the same PR as the v0.2.0 gitlink bump:

- Update `apps/web/src/types/material-schema.ts` with the new
  optional shapes.
- Extend `ComputedPropertiesSection.tsx` with:
  - "Mechanical" subsection: bulk/shear modulus + fragility flag.
  - "Surface" subsection: surface energy + work function (with
    cathode-activity tooltip for MES catalysts).
- Add a new `RelatedPapersSection.tsx` rendering the cross-reference
  summary — this is the UI surface for the novel MES capability.
- Add a new `SubstitutionCandidatesSection.tsx` — a compact list of
  alternative materials with rationale.
- No new Prisma tables required for v0.2; all data comes from the
  static fixtures. Prisma MaterialProperty migration continues to
  be deferred until we have reason to query materials server-side.

## Out of scope for v0.2 (deferred to v0.3+)

- **Adsorption energies for MES-relevant species** (H*, OH*, O*, NH₄⁺,
  Na⁺) — high compute cost; planned for v0.3.
- **Dielectric + piezoelectric tensors** — low MES relevance for v0.2
  materials (carbon + metals + simple oxides); defer until we cover
  more polar catalyst materials.
- **Robocrystallographer descriptions + computed XRD patterns** — low
  cost to fetch, no blocking reason to delay; add in v0.2.1 if time
  permits, otherwise v0.3.
- **C2DB / OPTIMADE federation** — requires a new data source; v0.3
  after v0.2 proves the schema-bump cadence works.
- **MACE-MPA structural embeddings** — MESS-Learning track; see the
  top-level strategy doc.
- **Composite scores (EVS, BCS, LSS)** — originally scheduled for
  v0.2 in the strategy doc, but v0.2 prioritizes data coverage
  first. Scores land in v0.3 once all their inputs (elasticity,
  surface, Pourbaix, paper evidence) are stably available.

## Coordination signals

- `data/mess-parameters-lock.json` continues to record which
  MESS-Parameters tag we're pinned against. Paper cross-reference
  (#5) has a tight dependency on this pin — a MESS-Parameters
  bump between our fetch and our ship will silently drift the
  corpus counts. Lock the pin at the start of v0.2 work and refuse
  to bump until v0.2.0 ships.

## Timeline estimate

- Phase 1 (API fetches): 1 day (mostly waiting on MP rate limits;
  8 elasticity + 8 surface calls trivial).
- Phase 2 (MXene + stainless curation): 1–2 days — real materials
  science judgment, not rote lookups. User review before committing
  the slug-to-mp.yaml update.
- Phase 3 (joins): 2 days — paper cross-reference needs a coverage
  pass and likely scope adjustment based on what's in the
  MESS-Parameters CSVs.
- Phase 4 (assembly + schema): 0.5 day.
- Phase 5 (v0.2.0 tag + messai-ai consumer PR): 0.5 day.

Total: ~5 working days at focused pace.
